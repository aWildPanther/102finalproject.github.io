Real-world Technologies

Home automation

Home automation is a real-world example of the Internet of Things. Home automation includes smart thermostats, smart appliances, smart home security, lights, and much more. These technologies are connected to the internet. They allow users to control and automate these devices using smartphones or other similar devices. Smart lights can be bulbs that can be dimmed, turned on/off, and color-changed through an app. Home security can use cameras throughout a house or property to provide live video feeds and motion detection alerts. Smart speakers are voice-activated assistants that can control various connected devices. Smart appliances can be controlled from anywhere in a house.

Smart agriculture

Smart agriculture connects physical and virtual components of the agricultural system to the internet to improve farming management. Farmers use smart agriculture to monitor and manage crops and livestock. IoT in agriculture helps farmers save time and money by reducing production losses and improving the health and yield of their crops. Smart agriculture technologies include sensors for monitoring soil, weather, crop health, and livestock tracking.
